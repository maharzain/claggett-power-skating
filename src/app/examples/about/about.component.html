COACH AMY CLAGGETT 

Coach Claggett has been surrounded by hockey her entire life. Since being introduced to roller hockey in Michigan at a young age she developed a love for the sport. Although after playing four years of roller hockey, she found her true passion, ice hockey. 

Coach Claggett began training constantly to be able to become the best she could as a skater when she lived in Washington DC and worked at the Fairfax Ice Arena. While working at the Fairfax Ice Arena she met the Weiss Family who influenced her drive. Michael Weiss,an olympic figure skater, was Coach Claggett’s earliest influence and gave her the tools and assistance needed to continue on her path to become a great player and eventual coach. 
​
Coach Claggett, as you can imagine, picked up skating quickly and began instructing mini power skating sessions at the age of sixteen. During this time, Coach Claggett was introduced to the Women’s National Team Coach, Kush Sidhu. Coach Claggett developed a strong bond with Coach Sidhu and he ended up becoming not only her coach but her mentor.

Coach Claggett played for the Washington Little Capitals (AAA) team from 1992-1996. During that time she was recruited to play in the 1995 US Women’s Ice Hockey Olympic development training camp. As a result of her hard work and dedication to skating, Coach Sidhu asked her to help coach CCM development camps. Coach Claggett ended up playing two years in Lake Placid, North Country College girls team, and practicing with the men's team.
​
After playing in Lake Placid, Coach Amy went on to play four years of ice hockey with The University of Maine-Orono, a D1 school for Women’s Ice Hockey and graduated with her bachelor’s degree in Exercise Science and Biomechanics of Sport. After Coach Claggett completed her undergraduate degree she began working for Sony and NBC in New York City as an Exercise Specialist. She then received a Graduate Assistantship from the University of Maine to further her education. While obtaining her Masters Degree, she studying exercise science and conducted research on the relationship between stride length and hamstring flexibility. 

After Graduate School, Coach Claggett was hired in Chicago by Peak Performance Health as an Exercise Physiologist for where she stayed for 11 years. Throughout that time she worked with a large range of clients from young athletes up to professionals. Coach Claggett’s main focus was helping these elite athletes recover from injuries and regaining maximum sport performance.  She went on to expand her knowledge by become a certified Kinesio Taping Professional.

In 2012, Coach Claggett moved to Waunakee, WI where she has been coaching in the greater Madison area now for over a decade. She has coached not only thousands of kids (AAA,AA)but many college( UW-Madison) juniors (Madison Capitals, Green Bay Gamblers), CCM Select Camps, and professional hockey players (AHL,NHL). 
​
Coach Claggett is not only a coach but an amazing mother to her three children, Liam (8), Emry (6) and Finn (3).
